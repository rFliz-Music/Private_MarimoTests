# Event Synthesis

The goal of these series of notebooks and little pieces of writing is to introduce a survey of perspectives on the authoring of procedural techniques for music composition. 
These techniques fall within the category of algorithmic music. 

We will motivate the techniques by grounding them in historical context.

### Freehanding it...
From music jams to video game music, non linear composition rules are the native language for art containers centered around emergent behaviour. And that really is the key isn't it... I don't really think I need to sing procedural art's praises but what the hell.
With this kind of approach the artist and the "consumer" (often the same person) get more bang for their buck so to speak... with a small amount of information (e.g. a handful of general principles) we are able to derive joy from the exploration of the extent of the implications of the system, an information structure whose scale vast outnumbers that of the principles (axioms)... this is one of the aspects that makes mathematics such a joyful endeavor. 

There are many types of approaches to procedural art but for the current process we'll focus on Stochastic and Parametric (vector field differential modeling) techniques.

By allowing an aspect of our piece to be Stochastic, we trade specificity for generality... be that as it may, this allows us to artistically speak about the uncertain... just a side tangent, while I do love how much the idea that stochastic music can serve to "minimize boredom" in something like "video game music" by avoiding direct repetition over prolonged periods of time. This is true but it doesnt speak to the true depth of stochastic approaches... the idea is that the work of art beckons to be re-instantiated, different everytime an the extent of this variability becomes a shade on the semantic landscape that the piece is trying to convey.
